Do Not Disturb · Microsoft Teams

If they're on DND in Teams,
don't send it.

Do Not Disturb doesn't mean "send it anyway, they'll see it later." It means do not disturb.

0 minutes of focus lost per interruption

Even with DND on, Teams still shows the badge

When you enable Do Not Disturb in Teams, notifications are suppressed — no sound, no banner, no pop-up. But the unread badge still appears: on the Teams icon in the Windows taskbar, on the activity bell, and on the chat thread itself.

T 1
Badge still appears on the Teams taskbar icon
even when the recipient has DND enabled.

The moment someone glances at their screen — even to check the time — that badge is visible. Research on attention residue shows that the mere awareness of an unread message pulls cognitive load away from the current task, often for ten to twenty minutes.

A notification badge is an unanswered question parked at the edge of someone's vision. They can't un-see it. They can't fully stop wondering what it says. The flow is already broken.

When someone sets their Teams status to Do Not Disturb — or when Teams automatically sets it during a Focus session via Viva Insights — they're telling you: "right now, the cost of an interruption is especially high." Sending a message into that window forces exactly the context switch they were trying to prevent.

Gilfoyle is deep in a kernel patch. The badge appears on his taskbar. He glances at it. Flow broken — before he's read a single word.

✓ What you should do
Richard 2:07 PM
Gilfoyle is on DND — reminder set for 3 PM
Gilfoyle Available
Richard 3:02 PM
Hey, got a quick question about the compression algo…

Richard waits. Gilfoyle finishes his Focus session and flips to Available. Richard messages then. No broken flow — and a much less withering response.

How to respect DND in Teams

  • ✓Check someone's status before you open a chat window.
  • ✓If they're on DND, set a reminder — type /remind in Teams or create a task for yourself.
  • ✓Ask yourself: is this truly urgent? If your message can wait an hour, it should.
  • ✓For genuine emergencies, use a phone call — which Teams DND can be configured to allow.
  • ✓Encourage your team to set DND and honour it by example — culture change starts with you.
The Rule

If someone is on DND in Teams, wait.
Set a reminder. Message them
when they're Available.

That's it. That's the whole rule.

In Teams you can type /remind to set a reminder,
or use Viva Insights to check when Focus sessions end.
